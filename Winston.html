Winston Churchill

"It is no use saying 'we are doing our best.' You have got to succeed in doing what is necessary."

1874-1965
  • 1874: Winston Churchill is born in Oxfordshire. His father is a well-known politician and his mother is an American socialite.
  • 1894: Winston Churchill graduated form the Royal Military College in Sandhurst. He was then appointed as a cornet in the British military.
  • 1895: Lord Randolph (Churchill's father) died at the age of 45. This incident resulted in the thought that Churchill must do as much with his life as he can.
  • 1899: Churchill campaigns for the Parliament as part of the Conservative Party. He lost the election but people were impressed with his skills.
  • 1900: Winston Churchill won a seat in the British House of Commons, and switched to Liberal Party four years later.
  • 1908: Winston Churchill marries Clementine Hozier.
  • 1940: Winston Churchill began his first term as prime minister, when he refused to negotiate with Germany.
  • 1941: Winston Churchill went to Washington D.C. to meet Preseident Roosevelt.
  • 1945: Winston Churchill lost the United Kingdom general election, and Clement Atlee tok over as the new prime minister.
  • 1951: Winston Churchill was elected prime minister again.
  • 1955: Winston Churchill retired as prime minister.
  • 1965: Winston Churchill died at the age of 90.
